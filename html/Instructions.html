Instructions

1. Game Overview

Players: 2–4

Play Time: 30–45 minutes

Recommended Age: 10+

The Siege of Hexfall is a fully cooperative board game in which players take the roles of Heroes defending the land of Hexfall from an ever-growing monster invasion. Monsters emerge from arcane portals and relentlessly advance toward villages and heroes. Players must coordinate movement, manage risk, and make sacrifices to seal portals and defeat the Monster Boss.

There is no Monster player. All monster actions are controlled by the game rules.

2. Objective

Heroes Win If:

  • All Portals are sealed
  • The Monster Boss is defeated

Heroes Lose If:

  • All Villages are destroyed
  • All Heroes are defeated

3. Components

3.1 Board Components

  • 19 Modular hex tiles
  • 3 Portal tiles
  • 3 Village tiles
  • 3 Ruin tiles
  • 10 Plain tiles

3.2 Structures

  • 3 Portal towers
  • 3 Village buildings
  • 3 Ruin structures

3.3 Heroes

  • Warrior miniature
  • Ranger miniature
  • Mage miniature
  • Barbarian miniature

3.4 Monsters

  • 8 Monster Grunts
  • 4 Monster Brutes
  • 1 Monster Boss

3.5 Tokens & Markers

  • 12 Health tokens
  • 6 Portal Seal markers
  • 6 Village Damage markers
  • 10 Loot tokens
  • 2 Rage markers (Barbarian)
  • 1 Round Tracker token

3.6 Dice

  • 1 six-sided dice (d6)

4. Setup

  1. Assemble the board using the 19 hex tiles in a hex-flower pattern.
  2. Place 1 Portal tile on three outer edge hexes.
  3. Place 1 Village tile adjacent to each Portal tile.
  4. Place 1 Ruin tile on three remaining inner hexes.
  5. Place the matching structure on each special tile.
  6. Each player selects one Hero and places their miniature on any Village tile.
  7. Place 2 Monster Grunts on each Portal tile.
  8. Set the Round Tracker to Round 1.

5. Heroes

5.1 Hero Stats

Hero Attack Defense Health Ability
Warrior 3 2 12 Shield Wall
Ranger 2 1 10 Ranged Shot
Mage 2 1 8 Arcane Seal
Barbarian 4 0 14 Rage

Heroes begin the game at full Health.

5.2 Hero Abilities

Shield Wall (Warrior): Once per round, reduce incoming damage by 1.

Ranged Shot (Ranger): The Ranger may attack a Monster on an adjacent tile without moving.

Arcane Seal (Mage): The Mage is more effective at sealing Portals and only takes 2 damage on a failed roll.

Rage (Barbarian): When the Barbarian takes damage, place 1 Rage marker on their base (maximum 2). Each Rage marker grants +1 Attack. At the end of the Barbarian’s turn, remove 1 Rage marker.

6. Round Structure

Each round consists of two phases:

  1. Hero Phase
  2. Monster Phase

After the Monster Phase, advance the Round Tracker by one.

7. Hero Phase

Heroes may act in any order. Each Hero takes one turn per round.

Hero Turn Structure

  1. Move – Move up to 2 adjacent tiles.
  2. Action – Choose one:
    • Attack
    • Seal a Portal
    • Loot a Ruin
    • End Turn

8. Special Hero Actions

8.1 Sealing a Portal

Any Hero standing on a Portal tile may attempt to seal it instead of attacking.

Hero Success Roll
Mage 3–6
Other Heroes 5–6
  • Roll 1 die.
  • On success, place a Portal Seal marker on the Portal and all heroes gain 5 HP.
  • Sealed Portals no longer spawn Monsters.
  • On failure, the Hero suffers 5 damage.
  • A sealed Portal cannot be reopened.

8.2 Looting a Ruin

Roll 1 die:

  • 1–2: Heal 5 Health
  • 3–4: +1 Attack for the next combat
  • 5–6: +1 Defense for the next attack

8.3 Mage’s Last Stand

When the Mage is defeated, before removing the miniature from the board, the Mage may immediately choose one:

  • Final Seal: Seal one open Portal anywhere on the board.
  • Arcane Collapse: All open Portals spawn one fewer Monster for the rest of the game (minimum 0).

9. Combat

  1. Attacker rolls 1 die and adds their Attack value.
  2. Defender subtracts their Defense value.
  3. Remaining value is dealt as damage (minimum 0).
  4. Remove Health tokens accordingly.

10. Monsters

Monster Attack Defense Health
Grunt -2 0 2
Brute 0 1 4
Boss 2 2 8

11. Monster Phase (Game-Controlled)

Step 1 – Spawn

  • If no Portals are sealed, each open Portal spawns 1 Grunt.
  • If one Portal is sealed, each remaining open Portal spawns 1 Brute instead.
  • Boss Round Exception: During a Boss round, only the Boss spawns. No Grunts or Brutes spawn from any Portals.

Step 2 – Target Priority

Monsters target in the following order:

  1. Adjacent Hero
  2. Nearest Hero
  3. Nearest Village

Step 3 – Move

Monsters move 1 tile toward their target.

Step 4 – Attack

  • Monsters attack if on the same tile as their target.
  • Resolve Monster attacks in the following order: Grunts → Brutes → Boss.
  • If multiple Monsters and multiple Heroes are on the same tile, each Monster attacks once. Heroes may choose how to assign Monster attacks among Heroes on that tile.
  • Villages take 1 damage per attacking Monster.

12. Boss Phase

Boss Spawn

When two Portals are sealed, spawn the Boss at the remaining open Portal. If no Portals remain, spawn the Boss at the nearest Ruin.

Boss Rules

  • Moves up to 2 tiles per Monster Phase
  • Attacks all Heroes on its tile
  • Ignores Village Defense

13. Villages

  • Each Village has 3 Health.
  • Place a Village Damage marker each time it takes damage.
  • When a Village reaches 3 damage, remove the structure.
  • If two Villages are destroyed, the Heroes lose.

14. Hero Defeat

  • When a Hero’s Health reaches 0, they are defeated and removed from play.
  • The remaining Heroes may continue as long as at least one Hero remains.

15. Difficulty Adjustments (Optional)

  • Easy: Portals spawn Monsters every other round.
  • Hard: Each open Portal spawns +1 Monster of its current type.

16. Design Notes

This game emphasizes:

  • Cooperative strategy
  • Tactical positioning
  • Risk vs. reward decisions
  • Physical clarity through 3D components

17. End of Game

If the Heroes seal all Portals and defeat the Boss, Hexfall is saved.

If the Villages fall or the Heroes are defeated, Hexfall is overrun.

Thank you for playing The Siege of Hexfall.
